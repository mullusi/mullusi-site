Architecture

How Mullusi is organized

Mullusi separates the public website, product route, review service, operator workspace, proof status, and future domain engines so visitors can understand what exists now and what is still staged.

In plain terms: the site shows the product map first, then keeps technical proof and runtime status available for inspection.

01

Request

A person or system starts with work that needs planning, review, or a recorded result.

02

Review

Mullusi turns the request into a proposed action with visible status, limits, and context.

03

Record

The system keeps the reason, review state, and repair notes attached to the work.

04

Execute

Approved work can move through the product service when runtime evidence is ready.

05

Inspect

Visitors and operators can inspect status, proof boundaries, and public routes separately.

Product map

Public routes show the product shape without exposing private internals.

Mullusi is the company umbrella. Mullu is the first product route. Proof, Playground, Sciences, and Surfaces explain status, demos, future domains, and public routes. Product engines, dashboards, and operational data stay private until ready.

  • Public pages describe what visitors can inspect now.
  • Private product work is not presented as live customer access.
  • Proof and status pages carry the technical runtime boundary.
